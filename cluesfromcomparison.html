Clues from Comparison

"The other side is greener..."

In this article, the behaviour of COVID-19 will be compared with the Middle Eastern Respiratory Syndrome(MERS), a virus first identified in Saudi Arabia in 2012. COVID-19 is a highly transmissable virus with a low mortality rate, but MERS is a lethal virus with low transmission. The simulations below will compare their behaviour without any social distancing.

COVID-19 (no social distancing)

MERS (no social distancing)

Even though MERS might be more life-threatening, as shown above, its low transmission means that much fewer people contract this virus than COVID-19. So COVID-19 can potentially be more widespread and, in turn, more dangerous.
